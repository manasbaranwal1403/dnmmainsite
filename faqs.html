About



1. Why do the total releases on the homepage differ from the total tracks I submitted in the catalog?

The total releases displayed on the homepage reflect only the completed, released, or delivered tracks. In contrast, the catalog includes tracks that may be pending, approved, or rejected.

2. What are average listeners?

Average listeners refer to the mean number of streams you receive during each revenue report update period.

3. When will my total revenue be updated?

Your total revenue is updated approximately every 3 to 4 months.

4. How do I submit an album, EP, or mixtape?

To submit an album, EP, or mixtape, enter the title of the project in the "ALBUM NAME" field and list the individual track names in the "TRACK NAME" field. Ensure you select the appropriate release type—album, EP, or mixtape. You can submit multiple tracks from a project by providing details for each track individually, but make sure the album name and cover art are consistent across submissions.

5. What are ISRC and UPC?

ISRC (International Standard Recording Code) is a unique identifier for sound recordings, while UPC (Universal Product Code) is a barcode used for tracking products. Both codes are essential for distribution and sales tracking.

6. How do I shift tracks to DireNote Media from another distributor?

To shift your tracks, follow these steps:
First, ask if the takedown is possible for your tracks. If they confirm, hold off on requesting the takedown immediately. Request the UPC and ISRC codes for your tracks from the current distributor. Send us all track details, including the UPC and ISRC codes, so we can proceed with distribution. Once we distribute the tracks on our end, inform them to proceed with the takedown.

7. How quickly can I release tracks?

You can release tracks as fast as 2 days. However, the timeline generally depends on your subscription plan and the type of content—whether it’s original music or a free-for-profit beat.

8. What do the C and P lines mean?

The C line (Copyright) indicates the copyright owner of the music, while the P line (Phonorecord) identifies the owner of the sound recording. These lines are important for protecting your intellectual property.

9. What is YouTube Content ID?

YouTube Content ID is a digital rights management system that allows copyright holders to identify and manage their content on YouTube. It scans uploaded videos against a database of files submitted by rights holders, enabling them to block, monetize, or track videos that contain their music.

10. What is CRBT (Caller Ring Back Tone)?

CRBT allows mobile users to personalize the tone callers hear while waiting for their call to be answered. Instead of a standard ringtone, callers can listen to a selected song, providing artists with an additional revenue stream when users pay to set their favorite songs as their ring back tones.

11. How do I submit an audio file and album cover?

To submit your audio file and album cover, first upload them to a file-sharing service like WeTransfer or Dropbox. After uploading, copy the sharing link and paste it into the "New Release" form when submitting your music.

12. Why is my track submission not updated in the catalog?

It may take up to 24 hours for new submissions to reflect in the catalog due to the processing time required to review and approve the submitted tracks.

13. What are smart links, and how do I claim them?

Smart links are landing pages that aggregate links to various streaming platforms for your track. They allow listeners to access your music easily, and you can share these links anywhere. We provide a smart link for each track you release within 24 hours of its release.

14. When will I get my revenue report?

You will receive your revenue report every 3 to 4 months, providing a detailed breakdown of your earnings from streaming, sales, and other sources.

15. Where can I find my analytics report?

Currently, we do not have a separate page for analytics. However, each time you receive a revenue report, it will include the total streams for each of your tracks, allowing you to track your performance.

18. How do I add a featuring artist in track submission?

To add a featuring artist, separate their name with "ft." followed by a comma. This format should also be used for the Spotify link.
